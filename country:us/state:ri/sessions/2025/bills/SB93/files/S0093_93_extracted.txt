A N A C T
POLICE FORCE AND FIREFIGHTERS
Introduced By: Senators Ciccone, Burke, LaMountain, and Dimitri
Date Introduced: January 31, 2025
Referred To: Senate Finance
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Sections 45-21.2-2 and 45-21.2-5 of the General Laws in Chapter 45-21.2
2 entitled "Optional Retirement for Members of Police Force and Firefighters" are hereby amended
3 to read as follows:
4 45-21.2-2. Definitions.
5 As used in this chapter, the words defined in § 45-21-2 have the same meanings stated in
6 that section except that “employee” means any regular and permanent police official or officer and
7 any regular and permanent fire fighter. The retirement board shall determine who are employees
8 within the meaning of this chapter; and “final compensation” means for members who retire on or
9 prior to June 30, 2012, the average annual compensation, pay, or salary of a member for services
10 rendered during the period of three (3) consecutive years within the total service of the member
11 when that average was highest. Effective on and after July 1, 2012 2025, “final compensation”
12 means the average annual compensation of a member for services rendered during the period of the
13 highest five (5) three (3) consecutive years within the total service of the member, and
14 compensation shall be defined in accordance with § 36-8-1(8). For members eligible to and who
15 retire on or after July 1, 2024, “final compensation” means the average of the highest three (3)
16 consecutive years of compensation within the total service when the final compensation was the
17 highest. Notwithstanding the prior sentence, in no event shall a member’s final compensation be
18 less than the member’s final compensation on or before June 30, 2012.
1 45-21.2-5. Retirement on service allowance.
2 (a) Retirement of a member on a service retirement allowance for members eligible to retire
3 on or before June 30, 2012, shall be made, subject to paragraph (a)(11) below, by the retirement
4 board as follows:
5 (1) Any member who has attained or attains age seventy (70) shall be retired as stated in §
6 45-21-16 subject to the discretions contained in that section; provided, that any member who is a
7 member of the Woonsocket fire department who has attained or attains an age of sixty-five (65)
8 years shall be retired. Retirement occurs on the first day of the next succeeding calendar month in
9 which the member has attained the age of sixty-five (65) years.
10 (2) Any member may retire pursuant to this subdivision upon written application to the
11 board stating at what time the member desires to retire; provided, that the member at the specified
12 time for retirement has attained an age of fifty-five (55) years and has completed at least ten (10)
13 years of total service, and notwithstanding that the member may have separated from service.
14 (3) Any member may retire pursuant to this subdivision upon written application to the
15 board stating at what time the member desires to retire; provided, that the member at the specified
16 time for retirement has completed at least twenty-five (25) years of total service, and
17 notwithstanding that the member may have separated from service.
18 (4) Any member may retire pursuant to this subdivision upon written application to the
19 board stating at what time the member desires to retire; provided, that the member at the specified
20 time for retirement has attained an age of fifty (50) years and has completed at least twenty (20)
21 years of total service, notwithstanding that the member may have separated from service; provided,
22 that the service retirement allowance, as determined according to the formula provided in § 45-
23 21.2-6, is reduced one-half of one percent (1/ %) for each month that the age of the member is less
2
24 than fifty-five (55) years.
25 (5) Any member of the South Kingstown police department may retire pursuant to this
26 subdivision upon written application to the board stating at what time the member desires to retire;
27 provided, that the member at the specified time for retirement has earned a service retirement
28 allowance of fifty percent (50%) of final compensation pursuant to § 45-21.2-6.1.
29 (6) Any member of the Johnston police department may retire pursuant to this subdivision
30 upon written application to the board stating at what time the member desires to retire; provided,
31 that the member at the specified time for retirement has earned a service retirement allowance of
32 fifty percent (50%) of final compensation pursuant to § 45-21.2-6.2.
33 (7) Any member of the Cranston fire department hired after July 1, 1995, or any member
34 of the Cranston fire department with five (5) years or less of service effective July 1, 1995, may

3 a service retirement allowance of fifty percent (50%) of final compensation for at least twenty (20)
4 years service; final compensation for Cranston fire department members is based on the
5 compensation components of weekly salary, longevity and holidays with longevity of the members

7 payment compounded each year on January 1st following the year of retirement and continuing on
8 an annual basis on that date; further, any illness or injury not covered in title 45 relating to the
9 presumption of disability is governed by the collective bargaining agreement between the City of
10 Cranston and members of the Cranston fire department.
11 (8) Any member of the Cranston police department hired after July 1, 1995, or any member
12 of the Cranston police department with five (5) years or less of service effective July 1, 1995, may
13 retire pursuant to this subdivision upon written application to the board stating at what time the
14 member desires to retire; provided, that the member at the specified time for retirement has earned
15 a service retirement allowance of fifty percent (50%) of final compensation for at least twenty (20)
16 years service; final compensation for Cranston police department members is based on the
17 compensation components of weekly salary, longevity and holidays with longevity of the members
18 highest year of earnings and members shall receive a three percent (3%) escalation of their pension
19 payment compounded each year on January 1st following the year of retirement and continuing on
20 an annual basis on that date; further, any illness or injury not covered in title 45 relating to the
21 presumption of disability is governed by the collective bargaining agreement between the City of
22 Cranston and members of the Cranston police department.
23 (9) Any member of the Hopkinton police department may retire pursuant to this subdivision
24 upon written application to the board stating at what time the member desires to retire; provided,
25 that the member at the specified time for retirement has earned a service retirement allowance of
26 fifty percent (50%) of final compensation for at least twenty (20) years service; final compensation
27 for Hopkinton police department members is based on the compensation components of weekly
28 salary, longevity and holidays with longevity of the members highest year of earnings and members
29 shall receive a three percent (3%) escalation of their pension payment compounded each year on
30 January 1st following the year of retirement and continuing on an annual basis on that date.
31 (10) Any member of the Richmond police department may retire pursuant to this

34 allowance of fifty percent (50%) of final compensation for at least twenty-two (22) years’ service

1 pursuant to § 45-21.2-6.3.
2 (11) Notwithstanding any provision in this section to the contrary, for any service on or
3 after July 1, 2012, final compensation shall be defined in accordance with § 45-21.2-2, and no
4 benefit adjustments shall be provided except as set forth in § 45-21-52(c).
5 (12) Notwithstanding any provisions of this section to the contrary, with respect to police
6 officers employed by the town of Johnston, only those police officers hired on or after July 1, 2010,
7 shall be eligible to be members of the Municipal Employees’ Retirement System of the state of
8 Rhode Island in accordance with this chapter.
9 (b) Retirement of a member on a service retirement allowance eligible to retire on and after
10 July 1, 2012, shall be made by the retirement board as follows:
11 (1) Any member may retire pursuant to this subdivision upon written application to the
12 board stating at what time the member desires to retire; provided, that the member at the specified
13 time for retirement attained the age of at least fifty-five (55) years and has completed at least
14 twenty-five (25) years of total service, and notwithstanding that the member may have separated
15 from service; or
16 (2) Effective July 1, 2015, the member makes contributions to the plan effective July 1,
17 2015, in accordance with § 45-21.2-14, and (i) The member at the specified time for retirement
18 attained the age of at least fifty (50) years and has completed at least twenty-five (25) years of total
19 service; or (ii) The member has completed at least twenty-seven (27) years of total service
20 regardless of the member’s attained age, and notwithstanding that the member may have separated
21 from service; or (iii) Effective as of July 1, 2025, the member has attained the age of at least fifty-
22 seven (57) years and has completed at least twenty (20) years of total service.
23 (3) Any member with contributory service on or after July 1, 2012, who has completed at
24 least five (5) years of contributory service but who has not completed twenty-five (25) years of
25 service, shall be eligible to retire upon the attainment of the member’s Social Security retirement
26 age.
27 (4) If a member had ten (10) or more years of contributory service and attained age forty-
28 five (45) prior to July 1, 2012, and would have been eligible to retire at or prior to age fifty-two
29 (52) in accordance with the rules in effect prior to July 1, 2012, the member may retire upon
30 attainment of age fifty-two (52).
31 (5) Effective July 1, 2015, a member who has completed twenty (20) or more years of total
32 service who has attained an age within five (5) years of the eligible retirement age under
33 subparagraphs (b)(1) or (b)(2) or (b)(3) or (b)(4) above, may elect to retire provided that the
34 retirement allowance shall be reduced actuarially for each month that the age of the member is less

10 (6) Notwithstanding any other provisions of this section, a member on June 30, 2012, may
11 elect to retire at his or her eligible retirement date as determined under the rules in effect on June
12 30, 2012, provided that a member making an election under this paragraph shall receive the
13 member’s retirement benefit determined and calculated based on the member’s service and final
14 compensation as of June 30, 2012. This provision shall be interpreted and administered in a manner
15 to protect a member’s accrued benefit on June 30, 2012.
16 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO TOWNS AND CITIES -- OPTIONAL RETIREMENT FOR MEMBERS OF
POLICE FORCE AND FIREFIGHTERS
***
1 This act, effective July 1, 2025, would require that "final compensation" for purposes of
2 calculating police and firefighter's retirement benefits, be based on the employee's highest three (3)
3 consecutive years compensation, rather than the current five (5) years and would permit police and
4 firefighters to retire, if they have attained the age of at least fifty-seven (57) years and have
5 completed at least twenty (20) years of total service.
6 This act would take effect upon passage.
