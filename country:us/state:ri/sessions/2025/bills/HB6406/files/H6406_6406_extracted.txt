S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
A N A C T
PREVENTION ACT OF 2016
Introduced By: Representatives Batista, Casey, and Shanley
Date Introduced: June 11, 2025
Referred To: House Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 21-28.9-4 of the General Laws in Chapter 21-28.9 entitled "The Good
2 Samaritan Overdose Prevention Act of 2016" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 21-28.9-4. Emergency overdose care — Immunity from legal repercussions.
4 (a) Any person who, in good faith, without malice and in the absence of evidence of an
5 intent to defraud, seeks medical assistance for someone experiencing a drug or alcohol overdose or
6 other drug- or alcohol-related medical emergency shall not be charged or prosecuted for any crime
7 related to the possession of a controlled substance or drug paraphernalia, or the operation of a drug-
8 involved premises, if the evidence for the charge was gained as a result of the seeking of medical
9 assistance.
10 (b) A person who experiences a drug or alcohol overdose or other drug- or alcohol-related
11 medical emergency and is in need of medical assistance shall not be charged or prosecuted for any
12 crime related to the possession of a controlled substance or drug paraphernalia, possession or
13 transportation of alcohol by an underage person, or the operation of a drug-involved premises, if
14 the evidence for the charge was gained as a result of the overdose and the need for medical
15 assistance.
16 (c) The act of providing first aid or other medical assistance to someone who is
17 experiencing a drug or alcohol overdose or other drug- or alcohol-related medical emergency may
18 be used as a mitigating factor in a criminal prosecution pursuant to the controlled substances act.
1 (d) The immunity related to the possession of a controlled substance or drug paraphernalia,
2 possession or transportation of alcohol by an underage person, or the operation of a drug-involved
3 premises afforded under this section shall also extend to a violation of bail, probation and/or parole
4 on those grounds.
5 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
