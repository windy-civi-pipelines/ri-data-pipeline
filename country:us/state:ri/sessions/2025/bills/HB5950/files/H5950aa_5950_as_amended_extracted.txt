A N A C T
RELATING TO TOWNS AND CITIES -- GENERAL POWERS
Introduced By: Representative Scott Slater
Date Introduced: February 28, 2025
Referred To: House Municipal Government & Housing
(by request)
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
2 amended by adding thereto the following section:
3 45-2-70. Reimbursement of public safety training expenses.
4 (a) Any law enforcement agency, fire department, fire district or emergency medical
5 services organization within the state that hires a public safety employee (including law
6 enforcement officers, firefighters, or emergency medical personnel) from another municipality or
7 fire district within the state shall, upon hiring such personnel, reimburse the original employing
8 municipality or fire district for the cost of training and equipping the employee, provided these
9 costs were incurred within three (3) years of the employee's hire.
10 (b) Reimbursable expenses shall include, but are not limited to:
11 (1) Costs for basic and specialized training programs;
12 (2) Uniforms, personal protective equipment, and other non-reusable items;
13 (3) Equipment and materials directly provided during the training period; and
14 (4) A prorated portion of the base salary and benefits paid during the training academy
15 period.
16 (c) The reimbursing organization shall notify the original employing municipality or fire
17 district within ten (10) days of the employee's hire. The original employer shall provide an itemized
18 statement of reimbursable costs within thirty (30) days of notification. Payment must be made
19 within one hundred twenty (120) days of receipt of the itemized claim.
1 (d) Reimbursement percentages are determined based on the time elapsed since the
2 completion of training:
3 (1) One hundred percent (100%) reimbursement for hires made within one year of training
4 academy completion;
5 (2) Seventy-five percent (75%) reimbursement for hires between one and two (2) years
6 after training academy completion;
7 (3) Fifty percent (50%) reimbursement for hires between two (2) and three (3) years after
8 training academy completion.
9 (e) This section supersedes any conflicting municipal policies, resolutions, charter
10 provisions or bylaws, or ordinances.
11 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

***
1 This act would establish a reimbursement system for a municipalities cost of training public
2 safety personnel when they are hired by a different municipality or public safety agency within
3 Rhode Island within three (3) years of receiving the public safety training.
4 This act would take effect upon passage.
