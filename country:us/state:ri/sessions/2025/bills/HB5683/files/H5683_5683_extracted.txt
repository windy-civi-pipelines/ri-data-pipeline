S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
A N A C T
ENTERPRISE
Introduced By: Representatives Giraldo, Alzate, Potter, Morales, Diaz, Biah, Felix, and
Tanzi
Date Introduced: February 26, 2025
Referred To: House Labor
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 37-14.1-6 of the General Laws in Chapter 37-14.1 entitled "Minority
2 Business Enterprise" is hereby amended to read as follows:
3 37-14.1-6. Minority business enterprise participation.
4 (a) Minority business enterprises shall be included in all procurements and construction
5 projects under this chapter and shall be awarded a minimum of fifteen percent (15%) twenty percent
6 (20%) of the dollar value of the entire procurement or project. Of that fifteen percent (15%),
7 minority business enterprises owned and controlled by a minority owner, as defined in ยง 37-14.1-
8 3, shall be awarded a minimum of seven and one-half percent (7.5%), and minority business
9 enterprises owned and controlled by a woman shall be awarded a minimum of seven and one-half
10 percent (7.5%). Annually, by October 1, the department of administration shall submit a report to
11 the general assembly on the status of achieving the aforementioned participation requirements in
12 the most recent fiscal year. Of that twenty percent (20%), minority business enterprises owned and
13 controlled by a minority owner, as defined in ยง 37-14.1-3, shall be awarded a minimum of ten
14 percent (10%), and minority business enterprises owned and controlled by a woman shall also be
15 awarded a minimum of ten percent (10%). The director of the department of administration is
16 further authorized to establish by rules and regulation the certification process and formulas for
17 giving minority business enterprises a preference in contract and subcontract awards.
18 (b) Any minority business enterprise currently certified by the U.S. Small Business
1 Administration as an 8(a) firm governed by 13 C.F.R. part 124 shall be deemed to be certified by
2 the department of administration as a minority business enterprise and shall only be required to
3 submit evidence of federal certification of good standing.
4 (c) The provisions of this chapter shall not be waived, including, but not limited to, during
5 a declared state of emergency.
6 (d) The state of Rhode Island will arrange for a disparity study to evaluate the need for the
7 development of programs to enhance the participation in state contracts of business enterprises
8 owned by women and minorities, to be repeated every five (5) years, beginning in fiscal year 2025.
9 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO PUBLIC PROPERTY AND WORKS -- MINORITY BUSINESS
ENTERPRISE
***
1 This act would increase the minimum required awarded to minority business enterprises
2 from fifteen (15%) percent to twenty (20%) percent and of that, require a minimum of ten (10%)
3 percent be awarded to minority owned businesses and a minimum of ten (10%) percent be awarded
4 to women owned businesses.
5 This act would take effect upon passage.
