S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
J O I N T R E S O L U T I O N
OFFICE OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES TO SUPPORT PHYSICIAN
REIMBURSEMENT RATE INCREASES IN THE RHODE ISLAND MEDICAID PROGRAM
Introduced By: Senators Bissaillon, DiPalma, Tikoian, Lawson, Sosnowski, Murray,
Urso, Britto, DiMario, and Burke
Date Introduced: March 21, 2025
Referred To: Senate Finance
1 WHEREAS, Adequate reimbursement for physicians and advanced practice providers is
2 critical to ensuring access to high-quality healthcare services for all Rhode Islanders; and
3 WHEREAS, More than half of Rhode Island voters report facing at least one healthcare
4 access challenge in the past year; and
5 WHEREAS, More than one third of Rhode Island voters had difficulty scheduling an
6 appointment with a specialist in the past year; and
7 WHEREAS, Rhode Island faces significant challenges in recruiting and retaining
8 physicians and advanced practice providers including, but not limited to, primary care providers,
9 pediatricians, and nurse practitioners, due to uncompetitive reimbursement rates; and
10 WHEREAS, Investment in increased physician reimbursement rates will enhance access
11 to healthcare services, improve health outcomes, and reduce long-term healthcare costs by
12 preventing avoidable hospitalizations and emergency room visits; and
13 WHEREAS, A targeted appropriation of the State’s general revenue funds will support
14 Rhode Island’s healthcare infrastructure and workforce sustainability; now, therefore be it
15 RESOLVED, That there is hereby appropriated, from the general revenue of the State of
16 Rhode Island for fiscal year 2025-2026, the sum of ninety million dollars ($90,000,000) to be
17 invested in increasing reimbursement rates for all physicians and advanced practice providers
18 including, but not limited to, primary care providers, pediatricians, nurse practitioners, and other
19 advanced practice providers; and the Rhode Island Executive Office of Health and Human
1 Services shall administer the appropriation and oversee the disbursement of funds to ensure that
2 reimbursement rate adjustments are effectively implemented across Medicaid and other
3 applicable State-funded healthcare programs.
