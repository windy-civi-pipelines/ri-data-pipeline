S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
A N A C T
RELATING TO TOWNS AND CITIES -- ORDINANCES
Date Introduced: February 26, 2025
Referred To: House Municipal Government & Housing
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 45-6-2 of the General Laws in Chapter 45-6 entitled "Ordinances" is
2 hereby amended to read as follows:
3 45-6-2. Imposition of penalties for ordinance violations.
4 (a) Town and city councils may impose penalties for the violation of ordinances and
5 regulations, not exceeding in amount five hundred dollars ($500) or imprisonment not exceeding
6 thirty (30) days in some jail or house of correction, and/or require restitution in cases involving
7 property damage or personal injury in an amount up to twenty-five hundred dollars ($2,500) and/or
8 require community restitution for a not-for-profit entity for not more than fifty (50) hours for any
9 one offense, to be prosecuted by some officer appointed for that purpose, and to be recovered to
10 the use of the town or city, or of the person or persons, and in the proportions, that the councils in
11 their ordinances and regulations designate.
12 (b) Whenever a municipality issues a fine in violation of an ordinance, those fines shall be
13 a lien against the real property within the municipality owned by the violator. If the fine is not paid
14 by the violator within ninety (90) days of issuance of the fine, the lien shall be recorded with the
15 records of land evidence of the municipality, and the lien shall incur legal interest from the date of
16 recording. The cost incurred by the municipality to remediate issues caused by the ordinance
17 violation, along with the unpaid fine, shall be added to the amount of taxes due on the real estate
18 where the violation was located and the tax collector of the municipality shall include those
19 amounts on tax bills to the property owner.
1 (c) The tax collector of the city or town shall have the same powers and shall be subject to
2 the same duties with respect to such claim as in the case of the annual taxes upon real estate, and
3 the provisions of law relative to the collection of annual taxes, the sale or taking of land for the
4 nonpayment thereof and the redemption of land so sold or taken shall apply to such a claim.
5 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

A N A C T
RELATING TO TOWNS AND CITIES -- ORDINANCES
