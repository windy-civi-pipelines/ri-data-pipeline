S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
S E N A T E R E S O L U T I O N
GRAY
Introduced By: Senators Sosnowski, Ruggerio, Zurier, Ciccone, Thompson, Quezada,
Patalano, Felag, Urso, and Murray
Date Introduced: January 14, 2025
Referred To: Recommended for Immediate Consideration
1 WHEREAS, It is with sadness that this Senate has learned of the passing of Walter J.
2 Gray, the beloved husband of Mary Gray for seventy years, and the father of Walt Gray and his
3 wife, Monica, and Tim Gray and his wife, Sheila. He was also the very proud grandfather of
4 eight grandchildren, Ryan and Matthew Gray, Abby, Kelly and Joseph Gray, Shannon and Kyle
5 Brassil, and Nora Gray; and
6 WHEREAS, Born in the City of Woonsocket, Mr. Gray was a son of the late John and
7 Alice Gray. He was a graduate of Mount Saint Charles Academy and served our nation with
8 honor in the United States Army. Upon returning from service, he earned his Bachelor’s degree
9 from Boston University in 1951; and
10 WHEREAS, Mr. Gray took a position as the assistant purser for Grace Line, Inc. before
11 joining the staff of the Providence Journal as a state staff reporter in 1952. In 1955, he and his
12 wife founded the East Providence Post weekly newspaper; and
13 WHEREAS, Well-respected for his journalistic talents, Mr. Gray was named press
14 secretary to Governor Christopher Del Sesto in 1959, and was appointed assistant professor of
15 communications at URI’s College of Agriculture in 1960. He subsequently took a position as a
16 public relations officer and later became Assistant to the President before taking a leave of
17 absence to become a foreign service officer assigned to New Delhi, India; and
18 WHEREAS, In 1968, Mr. Gray returned to the University of Rhode Island as the Director
19 of the Division of Marine Resources at the Graduate School of Oceanography. For his service to
1 URI, he received the URI President's Award for Administrative Excellence in 1978; and
2 WHEREAS, Always an active and contributing member of the community, Mr. Gray
3 served in numerous philanthropic, and State and community positions including President of the
4 Kingston Potter Trust of the R.I. Foundation, President of Christ the King Church Parish Council,
5 a member of the State of R.I. Trade Mission to Japan, a member of the Governor’s Council on
6 Tourism, an adjunct professor at Bryant University, former President of the Board of Directors of
7 the Courthouse Center for the Arts, and past President of the Narragansett Chamber of
8 Commerce; and
9 WHEREAS, The Honorable Walter Gray also served the people of Rhode Island in a
10 panoply of elected positions including as a member and past President of the South Kingstown
11 Town Council, and as a Rhode Island State Senator for two terms; and
12 WHEREAS, Walter Gray was a steadfast gentleman who left this world a far better place
13 because of his life’s endeavors. He enriched the lives of all who knew him and he is dearly
14 missed; now, therefore be it
15 RESOLVED, That this Senate of the State of Rhode Island hereby extends deepest
16 condolences on the passing of the Honorable Walter J. Gray; and be it further
17 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
18 transmit a duly certified copy of this resolution to Mrs. Mary Gray and Family.
19
