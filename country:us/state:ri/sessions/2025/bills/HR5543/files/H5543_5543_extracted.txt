LC001827

S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
H O U S E R E S O L U T I O N
Introduced By: Representatives Shekarchi, Blazejewski, Solomon, McNamara, Bennett,
Shanley, Hopkins, Chippendale, Casimiro, and Kennedy
Date Introduced: February 25, 2025
Referred To: House read and passed
1 WHEREAS, This House was saddened to hear of the passing of Ann McCaffrey, the
2 beloved wife of the late Eugene McCaffrey, Jr., the former Mayor of Warwick, and the cherished
3 mother of the Honorable Mary McCaffrey, our dear friend and former Senate Majority Leader
4 Michael McCaffrey, Esq. and his wife, Deirdre, John McCaffrey, Esq. and his wife, Margaret,
5 Eugene McCaffrey, III, and William McCaffrey and his wife, Heather; and
6 WHEREAS, Born in the City of Providence, Ann Davis was the eldest daughter of the
7 late F. Sheldon and Gertrude Davis, the sister of Elizabeth Alfonso and the late Gertrude Audet,
8 and the aunt to many nieces and nephews; and
9 WHEREAS, Ann grew up in the Conimicut neighborhood of Warwick. She graduated
10 from Gorton High School, earned her Bachelor’s degree from Rhode Island College, and attained
11 a Master of Arts degree in History from Providence College; and
12 WHEREAS, With a loving and kind personality, Mrs. McCaffrey created and nurtured a
13 marriage that flourished, and a home that was warm and welcoming to her large and boisterous
14 family. She was always encouraging and doted on her family, particularly in her role as “Nannie”
15 to her treasured grandchildren, Michael, Cailin, Brenna, Catherine, Deirdre, Brendan, Matthew,
16 Luke and Elizabeth, who were the light of her life; and
17 WHEREAS, For many years, Mrs. McCaffrey was an elementary and secondary special
18 education teacher in the Warwick Public Schools and was later a member of the RI Retired
19 Teachers' Association; and
20 WHEREAS, Devoted to her faith, Mrs. McCaffrey was a life-long communicant of St.
1 Benedict Church. In addition, she was a member of the National Society Daughters of the
2 American Revolution, General Nathanael Greene-Pettaquamscutt Chapter, The General Society
3 of Mayflower Descendants, and the Squantum Association; and
4 WHEREAS, Ann McCaffrey was a loving wife, mother and grandmother, a loyal and
5 lifelong friend to many, and a “ray of sunshine” to her family and all who knew her, and there is
6 no doubt that she left an indelible mark on the lives of those she knew and loved, and she is
7 dearly missed; now, therefore be it
8 RESOLVED, That this House of Representatives of the State of Rhode Island hereby
9 extends deepest sympathy and condolences on the passing of Ann D. McCaffrey; and be it further
10 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
11 transmit duly certified copies of this resolution to the Honorable Mary E. McCaffrey and Family,
12 the Honorable Michael McCaffrey, Esq. and Family, John McCaffrey, Esq. and Family, Eugene
13 McCaffrey, III, and William McCaffrey and Family.
========
