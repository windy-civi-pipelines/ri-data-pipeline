S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
H O U S E R E S O L U T I O N
THE STATE OF RHODE ISLAND
Introduced By: Representatives McGaw, Shekarchi, Blazejewski, Chippendale, Carson,
Cortvriend, Speakman, Spears, Boylan, and Ajello
Date Introduced: April 24, 2025
Referred To: House read and passed
1 WHEREAS, First held on April 22, 1970, Earth Day was created by Senator Gaylord
2 Nelson to bring national attention and support for environmental protection; and
3 WHEREAS, The very first Earth Day brought twenty million Americans from myriad
4 United States cities out to demonstrate. In 2025, one billion people in more than 192 countries are
5 expected to be involved in a wide range of activities coordinated globally; and
6 WHEREAS, This year marks the 55th Anniversary of Earth Day and is centered on the
7 theme "Our Power, Our Planet," emphasizing the collective responsibility to transition to
8 renewable energy sources and create a sustainable future, with a goal to triple global renewable
9 energy generation by 2030; and
10 WHEREAS, Earth Day has already had a significant and positive impact on our World
11 with 250 million trees planted, educators teaching about environmental issues in 149 countries, 7
12 billion Green School Grants, 2.7 billion Acts of Green, and 36 million cleanup volunteers; and
13 WHEREAS, Earth Day commands a collective voice from our world to encourage
14 governments, businesses and individuals "to acknowledge and rectify their roles in the
15 environmental crisis"; and
16 WHEREAS, Earth Day is an opportunity for "individuals from all backgrounds and
17 walks of life to unite in a global effort to advance sustainability and climate action"; now,
18 therefore be it
19 RESOLVED, That this House of Representatives of the State of Rhode Island hereby
1 celebrates the 55th Anniversary of Earth Day on April 22, 2025, and calls upon all Rhode
2 Islanders to educate themselves on the environmental hazards facing the Earth and our future, and
3 become part of the solution; and be it further
4 RESOLVED, That the Secretary of State be and hereby is authorized and directed to
5 transmit duly certified copies of this resolution to Lou Vergato, Executive Director, the Rhode
6 Island Resource Recovery Corporation, and Terrence Gray, Director, Rhode Island Department of
7 Environmental Management.
