S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
A N A C T
FOR CHILDREN WITH SPECIAL HEALTH CARE NEEDS
Introduced By: Representatives Stewart, Alzate, Cruz, Shallcross Smith, Kislak, Potter,
McGaw, and Casimiro
Date Introduced: February 28, 2025
Referred To: House Health & Human Services
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 23-13-9 of the General Laws in Chapter 23-13 entitled "Maternal and
2 Child Health Services for Children with Special Health Care Needs" is hereby amended to read as
3 follows:
4 23-13-9. Licensing and regulation of midwives — Penalty for violations.
5 (a) The state director of health is authorized and directed to make rules for the regulation
6 of the practice of midwifery and for the licensing of midwives, including regulations that require
7 the payment of an initial application fee equal to that fee that is imposed upon applicants for
8 licensure as nurse practitioners and a renewal fee also equivalent to that for nurse practitioners. The
9 rules regarding the regulation of the practice of midwifery shall authorize a licensed, certified
10 nurse-midwife (CNM) or a licensed, certified midwife (CM) to prescribe medications that might
11 be reasonably required by his or her patients; provided however, that a licensed, certified nurse-
12 midwife (CNM) or a licensed, certified midwife (CM) shall prescribe any of those medications as
13 authorized by the director of health. The provisions of this section relating to prescription authority
14 by certified nurse-midwives and certified midwives shall be overseen by the department of health
15 as defined in the department’s rules and regulations for licensing of midwives (R23-13MID). No
16 person not a licensed midwife or a physician, registered under the provisions of chapter 37 of title
17 5, shall practice midwifery, or shall make a practice of attending women in childbirth for hire, or
18 use the name or title of midwife. Any person who violates the provisions of this section, or who
1 violates any of the rules of the department of health made in pursuance of this section, shall be
2 fined not more than one hundred dollars ($100), or imprisoned not more than six (6) months, or
3 both, and the director of health may revoke the license issued to any person when, in the opinion
4 of the director of health, that person is guilty of unprofessional conduct.
5 (b) Scope of prescriptive authority for a certified professional midwife with prescriptive
6 authority under this section shall have the ability to order, obtain, carry and administer the following
7 medications in accordance with their training and practice:
8 (1) Lactation pumps and equipment;
9 (2) Nipple shields;
10 (3) Compression stockings and belts;
11 (4) Maternity belts;
12 (5) Diaphragms and cervical caps;
13 (6) Glucometers and testing strips;
14 (7) Iron supplements;
15 (8) Prenatal vitamins;
16 (9) Recommended vaccines;
17 (10) Prophylactic ophthalmic medication;
18 (11) Postpartum oxytocic;
19 (12) Vitamin K;
20 (13) Rho (D) immune globulin;
21 (14) Local anesthetic;
22 (15) Intravenous fluids and equipment;
23 (16) Sterile water for intradermal injections for pain relief;
24 (17) Magnesium sulfate for prevention or treatment of maternal peripartum seizures
25 pending transport;
26 (18) Epinephrine for use in maternal peripartum anaphylaxis resuscitation and neonatal
27 resuscitation;
28 (19) Measles, Mumps, and Rubella (MMR) vaccine to nonimmune postpartum individuals;
29 (20) Tetanus, diphtheria, acellular pertussis (Tdap) vaccine for use in pregnancy;
30 (21) Hepatitis B (HBV) birth dose for any newborn administration;
31 (22) HBIG and HBV for any neonates born to a hepatitis B positive gestational parent;
32 (23) Influenza vaccine;
33 (24) Any vaccines recommended by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
34 advisory committee on immunization practices for pregnant or postpartum individuals or infants in
LC001903 - Page 2 of 4
1 the first two (2) weeks after birth or pregnant or postpartum individuals;
2 (25) Terbutaline to temporarily decrease contractions pending emergent intrapartum
3 transport;
4 (26) Antibiotics for intrapartum prophylaxis of Group B (beta hemolytic) Streptococcus
5 (GBS);
6 (27) Antihemorrhagic drugs to treat postpartum hemorrhage including, but not limited to,
7 intravenous tranexamic acid, oxytocin, misoprostol, methergine, and prostaglandin F2 alpha;
8 (28) Nifedipine for indication of preterm labor pending transport;
9 (29) Antibiotics;
10 (30) Antiemetics;
11 (31) Antivirals;
12 (32) Antifungals;
13 (33) Low-potency topical steroids;
14 (34) Antipruritic medications and therapies;
15 (35) Galactagogues;
16 (36) Topical analgesia for anal, vulvar, and perineal pain;
17 (37) Preterm labor preventatives;
18 (38) Stool softeners;
19 (39) Vitamins and minerals for preventing and treating deficiencies;
20 (40) Over-the-counter medications as needed;
21 (41) Nonopioid medication for therapeutic rest;
22 (42) Medications for miscarriage prevention and completion;
23 (43) Smoking cessation;
24 (44) Prescription referrals for IV iron infusions;
25 (45) Intraosseous;
26 (46) Umbilical venous catheters for neonatal resuscitation;
27 (47) Intramuscular tranexamic;
28 (48) Oxygen and equipment;
29 (49) Rhogam;
30 (50) All-purpose nipple ointment; and
31 (51) Injectable Lidocaine.
32 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
