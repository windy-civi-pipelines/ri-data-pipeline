S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
A N A C T
PENETRATION
Introduced By: Senators Bell, Bissaillon, Murray, Valverde, Ujifusa, Mack, Euer,
Quezada, Kallman, and Acosta
Date Introduced: February 13, 2025
Referred To: Senate Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Title 11 of the General Laws entitled "CRIMINAL OFFENSES" is hereby
2 amended by adding thereto the following chapter:
3 CHAPTER 71
4 CUSTODIAL LAW ENFORCEMENT SEXUAL PENETRATION
5 11-71-1. Definitions.
6 The following words and phrases, when used in this chapter, have the following meanings:
7 (1) “Force or coercion” shall have the same meaning as in § 11-37-1.
8 (2) “Peace officer” shall have the same meaning as in § 12-7-21,
9 (3) “Sexual penetration” shall have the same meaning as in § 11-37-1.
10 11-71-2. Custodial law enforcement sexual penetration prohibited.
11 (a) A person is guilty of custodial law enforcement sexual penetration if he or she engages
12 in sexual penetration with another person; and
13 (1) He or she is a peace officer, and the victim has been seized, is detained, has been placed
14 in custody, has been placed under arrest, or is confined in a correctional or law enforcement facility
15 or vehicle; or
16 (2) He or she supervises the victim’s parole, probation, or community confinement.
17 (b) A person convicted of a violation of this chapter shall be punished by imprisonment for
18 a period not to exceed three (3) years.
1 (c) A person convicted under this chapter shall not be subject to the provisions of chapter
2 37.1 of this title.
3 (d) It shall be an affirmative defense that the act of sexual penetration resulted from force
4 or coercion by the alleged victim or victims.
5 (e) Any sentence imposed under this chapter shall run concurrently with any other sentence
6 imposed for the same case.
7 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO CRIMINAL OFFENSES -- CUSTODIAL LAW ENFORCEMENT SEXUAL
PENETRATION
***
1 This act would prohibit members of law enforcement from engaging in sexual penetration
2 with individuals in their custody. The act would also provide that a person convicted of custodial
3 law enforcement sexual penetration would face imprisonment for not more than three (3) years.
4 This act would take effect upon passage.
