5 46-4-6.2. Town of Bristol — Powers conferred.
6 (a) The provisions of § 46-22-14 or any other provisions of the general laws
7 notwithstanding and in addition to any authority and powers conferred upon the town council of
8 the town of Bristol, authority shall also be granted to the town council of the town of Bristol to
9 enact ordinances as the town council may deem necessary or desirable for the enforcement and
10 supervision of any rules and regulations established by the town council governing the public
11 waters and harbors within its jurisdiction, and to regulate by ordinance the speed, management, and
12 control of all vessels and the size, type, location, and use of all anchorages and moorages within
13 the public waters within the confines of the town, including, without limiting the generality of the
14 foregoing, the authority and power to regulate waterskiing upon any of the public waters; to
15 designate upon a map of the public waters within the town the places where permanent or temporary
16 moorings or anchorages may be maintained; to assign moorings; to remove moorings; to collect
17 fees for the use of moorings; to provide for minimum mooring specifications; to provide for
18 inspection of moorings; to provide regulations for houseboats that are not self-propelled as defined
19 in § 42-17.1-46; to provide regulations for regattas, races, marine parades, tournaments, and
1 exhibitions; and to provide for the removal of wrecks or derelict or abandoned boats or docks; to
2 provide for the regulation of skin diving and scuba diving; and to impose penalties for violation of
3 the ordinances not exceeding in amount one hundred dollars ($100) or imprisonment not exceeding
4 ten (10) days in some jail or house of correction for any one offense and the fines to be recovered
5 to the use of the town.
6 (b) No powers or duties granted herein shall be construed to abrogate the powers or duties
7 granted to the coastal resources management council as provided in chapter 23 of this title, as
8 amended.
