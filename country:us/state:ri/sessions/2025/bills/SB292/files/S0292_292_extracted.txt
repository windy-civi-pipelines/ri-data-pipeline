LC000669

S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
A N A C T
Introduced By: Senator Meghan E. Kallman
Date Introduced: February 13, 2025
Referred To: Senate Judiciary
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 13-8-2 of the General Laws in Chapter 13-8 entitled "Parole" is
2 hereby amended to read as follows:
3 13-8-2. Qualifications of board members.
4 The seven (7) electors to be named by the governor shall have the following qualifications:
5 (1)(i) One of the qualified electors shall be a psychologist or a physician who is
6 professionally qualified in the field of psychiatry or neurology;
7 (ii) One shall be a member in good standing of the Rhode Island bar;
8 (iii) One shall be a person who is professionally trained in correctional work or in some
9 closely related general field as a social work; and
10 (iv) One shall be a law enforcement officer; and
11 (v) One shall be a person formerly incarcerated or directly impacted by the criminal legal
12 system who has completed the probation and parole process three (3) or more years prior with a
13 background in one of the following areas: psychology, mental health and/or substance use,
14 transitional housing, re-entry after incarceration, education or law.
15 (2) All of the qualified electors shall be individuals who shall also have shown an interest
16 in social welfare problems.
1 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO CRIMINALS -- CORRECTIONAL INSTITUTIONS -- PAROLE
***
1 This act would require one elector of the parole board to be a person directly impacted by
2 the criminal justice system who has completed the probation/parole process three (3) or more years
3 prior to selection with a professional background in certain enumerated areas.
4 This act would take effect upon passage.
