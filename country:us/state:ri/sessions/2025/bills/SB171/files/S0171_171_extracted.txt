2025 -- S 0171
========
LC001053
========
S T A T E O F R H O D E I S L A N D
IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY
JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2025
____________
A N A C T
Introduced By: Senators Thompson, Lawson, Murray, Tikoian, Appollonio, Burke,
LaMountain, and Ruggerio
Date Introduced: February 05, 2025
Referred To: Senate Finance
It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Title 40 of the General Laws entitled "HUMAN SERVICES" is hereby
2 amended by adding thereto the following chapter:
3 CHAPTER 8.16
4 GRADUATE MEDICAL EDUCATION
5 40-8.16-1. Appropriations.
6 (a) Notwithstanding any provisions to the contrary, any funding appropriated by the general
7 assembly of the State of Rhode Island for the support of Medicaid graduate medical education
8 funding, all academic medical centers who provide care to the state's critically ill and indigent
9 populations shall be eligible for receipt of said funding. The executive office of health and human
10 services is directed to use any appropriated funds to support graduate medical education programs
11 and research in areas including, but not limited to, trauma, primary care, maternal health, mental
12 health and substance abuse.
13 (b) The secretary of the executive office of health and human services shall determine the
14 appropriate Medicaid payment mechanism to implement this program and amend any state plan
15 documents as required to implement the payments.
16 (c) Payments for graduate medical education programs shall be made annually.
1 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.

EXPLANATION
BY THE LEGISLATIVE COUNCIL
OF
A N A C T
RELATING TO HUMAN SERVICES -- GRADUATE MEDICAL EDUCATION
***
1 This act would allow the executive office of health and human services to use appropriated
2 funds to support graduate medical education programs and research. This act would also direct the
3 secretary of the executive office of health and human services to determine the appropriate
4 Medicaid payment mechanism in order to make the payments annually.
5 This act would take effect upon passage.
